Interactive Media Assignment 1 - James Warlond
Rich Brown's Website

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to within the experience was the website’s incredibly unconventional and unique design articulated by its loading page with flickering letters transitioning into the homepage with Rich Brown himself being front and center. It feels dynamic and unlike a typical website whilst displaying Brown’s vibrant personality and innovation. The blend of colour, typography and motion are enough to get the user to explore more of the website and as a freelance designer, that’s exactly what your website’s goal should be.

2. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Although I just stated that the homepage was extremely captivating, I actually ended up spending more time looking through his various projects, all of which are detailed and structured brilliantly. Each project showcase not only puts his works on display but it manages to reveal his design processes and decisions during his time working on these projects whilst maintaining an interactive format. There’s so many images and videos that are easy to get lost in, showing Brown’s utmost dedication to his work and it’s something I really respect.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most frequent action was scrolling through the various pages of the website, allowing me to observe his homepage and projects in full detail. Seeing as Brown’s website is so interactive and exploration-focused, it only feels natural to keep on scrolling and discovering more and more about his works and personality. Both vertical and horizontal scrolling are implemented which only adds to the immersion of the experience.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The website’s primary goal is almost definitely to showcase Brown’s various projects to employers and clients, though I also think it’s designed to present his creative and bright personality to anyone interested, employers and clients included. Every design choice within the website feels deliberate whilst remaining functional and it showcases skills in both graphic design and technical design, perfectly setting up future career opportunities.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Brown’s website communicates this goal of showcasing his plethora of projects to employers and clients through making his website innovative and dynamic. As you dive deeper into his portfolio, you can really tell how much dedication Brown spent to make the website eye-catching whilst making sure to highlight his personality. It’s bold, it’s colourful and it’s unique which is something any client is looking for in a designer.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think the website serves as an experience which can be repeated and looked back on by any user. On an initial run-through with how exploration-focused and large-scale the website is, it’s so easy to get lost in his mountain of interesting and different projects making it easy to miss details or even miss whole projects. Users can easily return to look through projects they might’ve missed on their initial experience which greatly increases engagement.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Brown’s website suggests repeat visits and experiences through unconventionality and captivation within its design meant to persuade the user to keep exploring. Additionally, as a designer rising within the design business, it’s only natural for his projects to increase over time which also encourages repeat visits in case his website has been updated to showcase anything new and unique.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The website’s main media forms is UX/UI design which Brown clearly specialises in, though it also refers to many additional media forms ranging from augmented reality, professional filmmaking and motion graphics, all of which he’s incorporated in his projects. These wildly different media forms really demonstrate Brown’s diverse skillset as a designer which perfectly sets him up for future career opportunities.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

To me, this website reveals that any user should be open to active engagement when interacting with an experience. These interactive elements and design decisions are made with user engagement in mind, and I think it’s important to respect that. It’s significantly more fun to interact with a website and soak in all the details rather than skimming through without a purpose.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

When engaging with an experience, curiosity is the most important aspect in my opinion as the more curious a user is, the more likely they are to spend more time within the experience. You want users to see your design as something brilliant rather than something ordinary which makes interactive elements and unique styling so important as they encourage users to explore with a sense of wonder.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

This could possibly be a personal issue but when interacting with Brown’s website, my cursor was hidden the entire time which made it tougher to click on various interactive elements. For a less technically experienced user I can see this issue being quite a bother since they likely don’t have the necessary hand-eye coordination to picture where their cursor is to click on an element, which reduces navigation and exploration.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

Far and away, the website’s most satisfying area is all of Brown’s project pages which are designed to be incredibly immersive and engaging. Every interactive element, whether it be a simple image or a more complex video, tells a different story and enhances the experience with Brown’s unique mold of typography, colour and motion graphics making scrolling through every page satisfying.
